Emergency Injunction Petition
1. Parties:
* Petitioners: Residents of the Cameron/Beach Boulevard Navigation Center.
* Respondents: County of Orange, Mercy House, and associated parties.
2. Facts:
* Residents have been exposed to contaminated soil and unsafe environmental conditions at the site.
* County approved the site despite known contamination and absence of public disclosure verifying protective measures.
* Asphalt cap and other remediation measures have not been publicly verified for effectiveness.
* Multiple residents have suffered severe health effects; deaths have been reported.
3. Legal Basis:
* Failure to protect human health constitutes imminent threat under California Code and public health regulations.
* Emergency injunctive relief is authorized to prevent irreparable harm.
4. Request for Emergency Relief:
* Immediate evacuation and relocation to safe housing.
* Medical screening and long-term monitoring.
* Independent investigation into responsible parties.
* Injunctive order prohibiting continued residence at the contaminated site.
5. Prayer for Relief:
* Petitioners request the court or authority to:
   1. Issue an emergency injunction ordering immediate removal of residents.
   2. Direct the County and involved parties to provide safe alternative housing.
   3. Ensure medical assessment and monitoring for all exposed residents.
   4. Investigate and hold accountable all responsible parties.
   5. Grant any other relief deemed just and proper to protect the health and safety of residents.
6. Declaration of Residents:
* Attached declarations confirm ongoing exposure, illness, and deaths.
* Residents attest to urgent need for immediate intervention.
Attachments:
1. Declaration of Residents (Anonymous)
2. Supporting Facts & Timeline
3. Deaths & Harm Statement
4. Emergency Medical Risk Statement

Attachment 1 – Declaration of Residents (Anonymous Group Declaration)
We, the undersigned residents of the Cameron/Beach Boulevard Navigation Center, declare as follows:
1. We have resided at the Navigation Center for extended periods, in some cases for years.
2. We were placed at this location by County-directed programs, including operations conducted by Mercy House.
3. We were not provided public proof that the site was safe for long-term human habitation at the time of placement.
4. We have experienced ongoing health symptoms consistent with environmental exposure, including respiratory distress, skin irritation, neurological symptoms, chronic fatigue, and other serious conditions.
5. We have witnessed repeated medical emergencies and deaths among residents since the site opened.
6. We believe continued residence at this site presents an imminent and irreparable risk to human life.
We declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct.
Executed on: ____________
Anonymous Group of Residents Cameron/Beach Boulevard Navigation Center
________________


Attachment 2 – Supporting Facts & Timeline
* The Cameron/Beach Boulevard Navigation Center was established to house unhoused individuals under County authority.
* The property was acquired with County funds.
* The site is known to have historical environmental contamination.
* Residents were placed at the site without publicly released proof of effective long-term protective measures.
* The City of Huntington Beach did not have authority over environmental safety approval for human habitation at this site.
* Mercy House operated the facility under County direction.
* Residents have remained at the site for years despite mounting health concerns and reported fatalities.
________________


Attachment 3 – Deaths & Harm Statement
Since the opening of the Cameron/Beach Boulevard Navigation Center, residents report numerous deaths and serious medical crises occurring on-site. These individuals will not be forgotten.
Residents assert that continued exposure to unsafe environmental conditions substantially increases the risk of cancer, organ damage, and premature death. The cumulative harm constitutes an emergency requiring immediate intervention.
________________


Attachment 4 – Emergency Medical Risk Statement
Medical risk factors associated with prolonged exposure to contaminated environments include, but are not limited to:
* Increased cancer risk
* Neurological damage
* Cardiovascular complications
* Respiratory illness
* Immune system suppression
Residents request immediate medical screening, relocation to safe housing, and ongoing monitoring. Any delay substantially increases the likelihood of irreversible harm or death.
